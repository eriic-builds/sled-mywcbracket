8. Alternative Approaches Considered

External GLTF avatars

Rejected: higher authored fidelity, but adds asset licensing, download, rig, loading, and maintenance surfaces that conflict with the local/no-build constraint.

Raise all polygon counts

Rejected: smooths the same capsule silhouette and spends GPU budget without fixing proportion or clothing shape.

Move only the captain

Rejected: without a camera truck and right-centered payoff, the carry exits composition and recreates side-biased framing.

Keep the centered V1 timeline

Rejected: it contradicts the supplied storyboard's left-to-right journey and later team payoff.

Parent the trophy to one hand

Rejected: fixes orientation for one wrist but makes the second hand and shared scene ownership unstable. The neutral carrier remains the correct two-hand authority.

Map appearance to country

Rejected: it creates stereotype-prone identity coupling. Generic rig variants own appearance; country owns only kit, flag, and celebration palette.

Duplicate the scene in the mock

Rejected: V1 already proved that a second renderer drifts from production.

Post-processing / tilt-shift

Rejected: adds cost, suggests miniature scale, and conflicts with the full-scale straight-on direction.

10. Learning Mode (ELI5)

Imagine a school play with a reliable stage manager. The lights turn on correctly, the curtain opens correctly, and everyone exits safely. The problem is that the clay actors still look rough and everyone stands in the wrong places for the new story. V2 keeps the stage manager. First it sculpts better actors, then puts the trophy on the left and the team on the right, then rehearses the walk and jump, then checks that the curtain and safety rules still work. The final rehearsal starts the captain walking while the old curtain is still fading, turns the trophy whenever the captain turns so both hands stay comfortable, gives the seven players visibly different hair, skin, and facial-hair treatments, keeps the waiting team planted, and fires the confetti while the trophy is still rising.

Anchor

A named invisible point attached to an object. Move the plinth anchor and the trophy animation knows where to go.

IK

Math that bends the shoulder and elbow so a hand reaches a target. It cannot make an arm truly longer.

Deterministic

The same timeline time always creates the same pose, even after scrubbing or replay.

Frontal truck

The camera slides sideways but keeps looking straight ahead because its position and target slide together.

11. What a Senior Engineer Would Notice
  • The highest-risk code is not the new geometry; it is the intentional rewrite of old art-direction tests.
  • The trophy seam was correctly abstracted, but pixel bounds still mattered; alpha padding is part of geometry when compositing DOM and WebGL.
  • The podium must be separated from teammate visibility before the storyboard can read.
  • The shortest captain owns the lift reach budget, and production/tests must share one pure reach helper.
  • A two-hand scene-space prop needs a neutral carrier for both position and orientation; then its rotated grip anchors become the IK targets.
  • The exact frame order matters: body → carrier position/quaternion → trophy → grips → arms → render.
  • Starting action behind an existing transition can improve pacing without weakening the transition or lifecycle contract.
  • Representation is testable architecture: seven hero variants should have an explicit, country-independent appearance spread.
  • More segments do not create authored anatomy; non-radial massing and overlapping transitions do.
  • A stadium reads full-scale through enclosure, openings, aisle rhythm, and roof depth before crowd count.
  • Effects should key off the trophy's physical apex, not wait for the next named phase.
  • Effect duration has two layers: the outer active window and each instance's local trajectory. Both must support the requested lifetime.
  • Performance-test extraction boundaries matter: scanning only the dispatcher missed a per-supporter array allocation inside a called helper.
  • Banner and hanging flag remain central stadium identity; payoff lights and confetti recenter on the right podium.
  • Phone should follow the story beat instead of showing every location at once.
  • Visual completion requires real dashboard evidence: only that path revealed the padded capture misalignment.
  • Preserving V1 history makes the reversal understandable rather than contradictory.

13. Lessons Learned

Protect good seams

The best refactor is often not touching an abstraction that already makes the change cheap.

Review stills before motion

Animation can conceal weak anatomy, bad contact, and misaligned stage geometry.

Tests encode taste

A composition assertion is valuable, but a new approved direction requires an explicit unlock and re-lock.

Reach is geometry

A solver can bend joints; it cannot make an impossible target physically correct.

One renderer compounds

Production-backed review means every fidelity and choreography correction is reviewed where it will ship.

Evidence closes the loop

Source checks, frame review, and lifecycle checks answer different failure modes; all three are required.

Pixels have bounds too

A transparent canvas can be positioned correctly while its visible object is not; composite using alpha bounds, not only element bounds.

Density is not design

Polygon count only smooths a shape; authored profiles, overlap, and material hierarchy make it feel intentional.

Time the event, not the label

Celebration effects belong on the trophy rise and apex, even if the broader payoff phase continues afterward.

Guard the real hot path

A dispatcher-only source check can pass while called helpers allocate every frame; include the complete frame-call chain.

Transform before solving

For a two-hand prop, apply both carrier position and quaternion before sampling grip anchors or running IK.

Overlap beats dead air

Starting the captain behind the protected curtain shortened perceived intro time without deleting continuity.

Make representation explicit

Country-independent variant contracts are safer and more reliable than hoping a larger palette produces a diverse hero frame.

Loop the trajectory, not the allocation

Continuous fireworks reuse bounded instances with deterministic local-time modulo; they do not append new meshes.
